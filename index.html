Sales Training

Coverage Script

Rapport Reminder:
Before diving into coverage — build rapport! Ask about their day, comment on something local, family, weekend plans, etc. A positive connection makes the whole conversation smoother.
Step 1: Introduction & Info Confirmation
A
Agent Good morning/afternoon [Prospect], this is [Your Name] — I'll be your licensed agent assisting you with those savings today.

How's your day been so far?

[Pause for rapport: “How’s your week going?” “You from around here?” “Any fun plans coming up?” etc.]

I’m going to confirm your information real quick so we can make sure you're getting the best possible rate.
(CONFIRM — don't make them repeat everything)
Step 2: Experience & Expectations
A
Agent So [Name], I'm pulling up your reports — that'll show me any claims or accidents in the last few years and where you're currently insured.

Looks like you're with [Current Carrier]. How has your overall experience been with them — rates, customer service, claims handling?
(Dig for pain points: call center, rate increases, no policy reviews…)
Key Question
While I pull your coverages up, I always like to ask my customers this:

“Let's say you're driving and get into an at-fault accident with large damage — how much of that bill would you expect your insurance company to cover?”
C
Customer All of it.
A
Agent Exactly — and that's why we always make sure our customers have strong coverage. Let's go over what you currently have with [Current Carrier] so you know exactly what you're protected for.
Step 3: Explaining Coverages
A
Agent First we have Bodily Injury Liability — this pays for the other person's medical bills if you're at fault.

Right now you have 30/60 — we'll match that.

Same thing with Uninsured Motorist coverage — it protects you and your passengers if the other driver has no insurance, not enough, or it's a hit-and-run.
Property Damage Liability (Most Important!)
A
Agent Now this is the coverage that really concerns me.

Property Damage Liability pays for the other person's vehicle/property if you're at fault.

You're currently at the state minimum — $15,000. Anything over that comes out of your pocket.

Quick example — are you familiar with a Ford F-150?
C
Customer Yeah.
A
Agent Do you know roughly how much a new one costs these days?
C
Customer Around $60,000.
A
Agent Exactly. So if you hit a brand new F-150 and total it, your current policy would pay $15,000…

…and you'd be responsible for the rest.

How much would that be out of pocket?
C
Customer $45,000.
A
Agent Right — and how does that sound to you?
C
Customer Horrible.
A
Agent Exactly! That's why I'm putting you at $300,000 in property damage — so you're fully protected.

Makes sense why we do that, right?
Step 4: Other Important Coverages
A
Agent Next is Collision — covers damage to your own vehicle no matter who's at fault.

I recommend a $1,000 deductible — higher deductible = much lower premium, and most people don't file small claims anyway.
A
Agent Comprehensive covers non-collision damage — theft, vandalism, fire, hail, deer, etc.

$250 deductible is usually best here — these claims tend to be more frequent and smaller.
Step 5: Experience Recap Close
A
Agent While the rate is calculating, let me explain how things work once you're set up with us…

We're a local agency — no call centers. You get direct access to me and our team.

We give you my cell for after-hours questions.

No teaser rates — your rate is locked based on full underwriting.

We review your policy 45 days before renewal to make sure you're always getting the best rate and discounts.

You said you want full protection — right now you're only covered to $15,000…

With us you're getting $300,000 — so you're never left with a $45,000 bill.

Your monthly payment for this full protection is $[amount].

Quick last questions: Who is the vehicle financed through?
And what card would you like to use for today's payment?

Objection Handling

Core Principle: Every objection loops back to — You don't have enough coverage right now… and we're fixing that.
I totally understand. You told me you wanted full protection, right?

Right now with [Current Carrier] you're only covered for $15,000 in property damage — we talked about the F-150 example where you'd be stuck paying $45,000 out of pocket… which you said you wanted to avoid.

That's why we set you up with $300,000.

So let's make sure you're never in that position. What's the most convenient day for you to make that payment?
Totally fair. You were paying less because you were getting less — only $15,000 in coverage.

Most people can find the extra $20–$60/month, but almost nobody has an extra $45,000 sitting around after an accident.

That's why we're giving you $300,000 in protection.

What's the most convenient day to make that payment?
Final Close (Short & Confident):

You're moving from minimum coverage to real protection — that's the whole point of insurance.

What card works best to lock this in today?

Cross-Selling

Goal: Assume interest. Link to value & discounts — not just price.

Auto → Home

A
Agent Okay great — my system is going to run a home quote as well. When was the last time you had the roof replaced?

Home → Auto

A
Agent Now that we've got your home squared away, I'm going to pull up your auto too so we can bundle them and unlock the multi-policy discount.

Who do you currently have your vehicles insured with?
