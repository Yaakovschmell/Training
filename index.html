Module 05Tips & Tricks

🧮 Quoting & Pricing

  • Run quotes strategically: If married, always run the quote in the spouse's name to see if the Insurance Score (IS) is lower.
  • Always ask who the mortgage company is when you ask if they have a mortgage — it will allow you to use mycoverageinfo to see their policy many times.
  • RCT drives pricing: Pricing is based more on RCT than rebuild amount.
  • RCT optimizations:
    • Foundation: concrete → block
    • Plaster → drywall
    • Doors → hollow core (not solid)
    • HVAC → Central AC/Heat
    • Vinyl siding instead of aluminum
    • Built-in garage: exclude from livable square footage
  • Market value rejection: Find the highest supported value (Homes.com usually works best).
  • Early signing bonus: Always make sure to have the early signing bonus on the home quote.

🛡️ Coverage & Discounts

  • Review current coverage: Use MyCoverageInfo.com to check deductibles, rebuild amount, and pricing.
  • Multi-policy savings: If they already have auto or home with Allstate, clearly explain how much the rate drops with bundling.
  • Household rule: If boyfriend & girlfriend live together, Allstate treats them as married.
  • Price-driven prospects: If you're higher, offer the budget option:
    • 30/60 limits
    • No PIP
    • $2,000 deductibles
    • No roadside or rental
  • Always offer to cancel: Never assume the customer will cancel their current insurance on their own.

📞 Follow-Up & Pipeline

  • Persistence wins deals: Do not call or text once and move on. Follow up consistently until they respond.
  • Revive ghosts: Go back through old texts from months ago and re-engage cold leads.
  • Daily cleanup: Check your Neglected folder every day for quoted leads.
  • Push for the real objection: Do not stop until the prospect gives the true reason they won't bind.
  • Before hanging up on a non-competitive prospect: Always check for upcoming factors that could change the price — things like a renewal coming up, a claim that's about to fall off or age out, an upcoming birthday, or a new vehicle. Set a follow-up for when it matters.

⚙️ Systems & Process

  • Never quote without required info: Always confirm a VIN before giving pricing.
  • Build rapport: Make sure you are in rapport and not just having a transactional conversation. Give them a reason to want to do business with you.
  • Call length check: If your call is over before 10 minutes, there's something we didn't do correctly or missed.
  • Missing license numbers: Start a quote in the driver's name or check for a prior Allstate policy.
  • Call recordings: If SMTP doesn't record, clear cache and retry.
  • DND during calls: Prevent split recordings by enabling Do Not Disturb.

Module 07Call Scripts
😊 Tone & Energy — Every Single Call

Your tone is the first thing a prospect reacts to — before your pitch, before your price, before anything else. Be friendly, be warm, and bring energy to every call. If you sound flat, rushed, or frustrated, they'll mirror it right back at you and the call is over.

Prospects will not respond well to an aggressive or negative tone — they stay on the line with people who genuinely sound like they want to help. Smile before you dial, use their name, and treat every call like it's your first one of the day.

📋 The Only Goal: Get the Callback

When calling internet leads who requested a quote, your voicemail has one job — to earn a callback. Keep it under 20 seconds, sound natural, and always remind them that they initiated the request. Bracketed text like [Agent Name] should be replaced with your own info before calling.
